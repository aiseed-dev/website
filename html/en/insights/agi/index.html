Structural Analysis 03

The AGI Mistake

There Is Infinite Work — Human Work Increases

2025.04.04

Structural Analysis Series "03"

The Linguistic Definition of "Work"

"AGI will take all our jobs."

This statement defines "work" linguistically — as tasks performed in offices, on computers, through language. Writing documents, analyzing spreadsheets, answering emails, coding software.

Yes, AI can do most of that. It already does.

But this definition of work is structurally wrong.

The Structural Definition of "Work"

Work that AI CANNOT do:

Soil regeneration → Rebuilding microbial networks in degraded farmland
Ecosystem restoration → Replanting forests, restoring wetlands, rebuilding biodiversity
Local food production → Growing food where people live
Infrastructure repair → Physical maintenance of water, roads, buildings
Elderly care → Physical presence, human touch, emotional support
Disaster response → Physical rescue, cleanup, rebuilding

These are not "low-skill" jobs that will be automated next. These are physical processes that require human bodies in physical environments. AI processes information in nanoseconds. Microbes reproduce on monthly timescales. Soil structure recovers over 1–3 years. Ecosystems stabilize over 5–10 years.

AI cannot coexist with microbes. The timescales are incompatible.

The Scale of Physical Work Ahead

33% of global farmland is degraded.

This means billions of hectares need soil biology restoration. Each hectare requires human observation, local adaptation, site-specific decisions about plant species, timing, and management. No two fields are the same. No algorithm can replace local knowledge.

Microbial network formation: months. Soil structure recovery: 1–3 years. Ecosystem stabilization: 5–10 years. Full restoration of degraded farmland: potentially decades.

This is not a problem that can be solved by scaling up a software platform. It requires billions of people, over decades, working with soil.

Six Types of Work AI Cannot Do

1. Soil regeneration Stop chemical inputs → Let microbes recover → Monitor and adapt. Every field is different. Requires human presence and judgment.

2. Local food production Grow food near where people live. Reduce dependence on global supply chains. Requires physical labor, local knowledge, seasonal adaptation.

3. Ecosystem restoration Replant native species. Rebuild wetlands. Restore biodiversity. Each ecosystem is unique. Requires hands in the soil.

4. Water infrastructure Repair and maintain local water systems. Build rainwater harvesting. Restore natural water cycles. Physical construction work.

5. Building with natural materials Wood, bamboo, hemp, mycelium, earth. Local materials for local needs. Requires craftsmanship, not automation.

6. Care work Elderly care, childcare, community health. Human presence is irreplaceable. AI can assist with scheduling and information — but cannot hold a hand.

The UBI Delusion

"If AI takes all jobs, we'll need Universal Basic Income."

This argument is built on the wrong premise.

The UBI logic (flawed):
AI takes all jobs → Humans have nothing to do → Government pays people to exist

The structural reality:
AI takes information jobs → Humans redirect to physical jobs → More work than ever
33% of farmland degraded → Soil to rebuild → Decades of work
Ecosystems collapsing → Biodiversity to restore → Centuries of work
Supply chains fragile → Local production needed → Infinite work

The problem is not "no work." The problem is that the work that needs doing is not valued by the current economic system.

Rebuilding soil does not generate quarterly profits. Restoring ecosystems does not produce shareholder returns. Growing food locally does not scale into a tech unicorn.

But this work is essential for survival.

AI as the Amplifier

AI does not replace human work. It amplifies human thinking.

The correct division of labor:

AI → Information processing, structural analysis, pattern recognition
Humans → Physical work with soil, plants, microbes, ecosystems

AI → Analyzes which crops win against weeds in which conditions
Humans → Actually plant, observe, adapt, harvest

AI → Identifies degraded farmland from satellite data
Humans → Walk the land, test the soil, begin restoration

This is exactly how this website was built. AI (Claude) processed information, wrote code, structured analysis. A human decided what to analyze and why it matters.

The same principle applies to farming. AI can tell you the optimal planting time. Only a human can put seeds in the ground.

AGI does not eliminate human work. It reveals how much work remains undone.
33% of the world's farmland is degraded. Ecosystems are collapsing.
Supply chains are fragile. Local food production barely exists.
AI handles information. Humans handle soil.
The work ahead is not less — it is infinitely more.
