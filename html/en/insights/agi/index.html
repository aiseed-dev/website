The AGI Mistake

Data Centers Run on Oil. How Many Geniuses Do We Need? Does Past Data Show the Future?

2025.04.04

Structural Analysis Series 06

Without Oil, Data Centers Stop

AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) will transform humanity's future — so the tech industry says. But few question its premise.

AGI runs on data centers. What are data centers made of?

Petrochemical materials essential to data centers: Circuit boards (PCB) → Epoxy resin + glass fiber (petroleum). No alternative. Semiconductor packaging → Epoxy mold compound (petroleum). No alternative. Photoresist → Photosensitive resin (petrochemical). The foundation of chip fabrication. No alternative. Server housings → ABS, polycarbonate (petroleum). Heat resistance and flame retardancy required. Cable insulation → PVC, polyethylene (petroleum). No bio-alternative meets flame-retardancy standards. Fiber optic coating → Acrylate (petroleum). Optical precision required. No alternative. Coolant hoses → Synthetic rubber, PVC (petroleum). Chemical resistance required. Anti-static flooring → Petroleum-based vinyl. Electrostatic discharge directly destroys equipment. No alternative.

These are consumables. Servers are replaced every 3–5 years. Cables degrade. Cooling systems wear out. Data centers continuously consume petrochemical materials.

AGI's material dependency:
Running AGI → Requires data centers
→ Data centers require semiconductors
→ Semiconductor fabrication requires photoresist
→ Photoresist requires petrochemical feedstock
→ Petrochemical feedstock requires naphtha
→ Naphtha requires crude oil refining
→ When oil runs out, semiconductors cannot be made
→ Without semiconductors, AGI does not run

If AI promises a future beyond oil, it must first face the fact that the infrastructure running AI itself depends on oil. This is not just an electricity problem. It is a materials problem. Even if renewable energy powers the data center, the server boards, chip photoresists, and cable insulation can only be made from petrochemicals.

How Many Geniuses Inside the Data Center Do We Actually Need?

Suppose the materials problem is solved and AGI becomes reality. The next question: how much work actually requires a genius trapped inside a data center?

AGI is a genius of language. A genius of information. A genius of analysis. But it lives inside a data center. It cannot enter the physical world.

What AGI can do What AGI cannot do
--- ---
Write documents Regenerate soil
Write code Plant crops
Translate languages Grow mycorrhizal fungal networks
Analyze reports Fix broken water pipes
Devise strategies Retrofit building insulation
Draft legal documents Restore degraded forests
Diagnose medical images Physically care for patients
Predict financial markets Channel water into rice paddies

Everything on the left is completed inside an office, inside a screen. Everything on the right requires human hands and feet in the physical world.

Structurally speaking: 33% of global farmland is degraded → Recovery requires human hands Vegetable cultivation without chemical fertilizer → Practice in the field must start from scratch Most urban infrastructure is aging → Distributed redesign and physical construction needed Ecosystems are deteriorating worldwide → Physical restoration of forests, rivers, wetlands needed This is work on a scale that billions of people cannot finish in decades.

Those who say "AI will take our jobs" define only screen-based work as "work." In the physical world, there is infinite work that AI cannot do.

Human work does not decrease. It increases. But it is no longer office work — it is work with soil, water, and ecosystems.

Why UBI Is Wrong

"If AI takes all jobs, Universal Basic Income will solve it." This argument stands on the same flawed premise.

But UBI has an even more fundamental problem. It assumes fossil resources are infinite.

UBI's hidden assumption: Distribute money → People consume → The economy keeps turning But what are the consumed products made of? Food packaging → Plastic (petroleum) Clothing → Polyester (petroleum) Electronics → A mass of petrochemical materials Logistics → Trucks running on diesel Running consumption through UBI means running petrochemical product consumption. Since fossil resources are finite, this premise does not hold.

The AGI + UBI logic (wrong on two levels):
AI takes jobs → Unemployment rises
→ Distribute money → Sustain economy through consumption
→ But consumed products are made from petrochemical materials
→ Fossil resources are finite → The consumption economy itself is unsustainable

And manufacturing the bio-materials that come after fossil resources requires massive human labor.

Human labor needed for bio-material production: Microbial cultivation → Building, operating, and managing cultivation facilities requires people Plant feedstock farming → Preparing land, growing, harvesting requires people Mycelium production → Substrate preparation, growth condition management requires people Biomass collection → Gathering and transporting agricultural waste is physical labor Quality control → Bio-derived materials have high quality variation. Human judgment essential

Petrochemical plants can mass-produce with few workers. Bio-materials, dealing with living organisms, are difficult to automate and labor-intensive. There is no room to let people sit idle on UBI. Bio-materials require human labor permanently — not just during the transition. Petrochemical plants, once built, run with few workers. Bio-materials are different. Microbes are alive. Plants change with the seasons. Quality varies every time. This is not a "transition period problem" — it is a permanent structure inherent to working with living organisms.

The problem is not "no work." The problem is that physical work that must be done is not valued.

Does Collecting Massive Past Data Have Any Meaning?

Another fundamental problem with AGI. Current AI learns by collecting massive amounts of past data and recognizing patterns. But can past data reveal the future?

Domains where past data is useless: Climate change → Past patterns no longer apply. Unprecedented extreme weather is the new normal. Geopolitics → Could past data have predicted the 2026 Iran war? It could not. Ecosystem collapse → Systems change irreversibly beyond thresholds. Thresholds are not recorded in past data. Soil microbiomes → Complex systems. Individual microbe behavior cannot predict whole-system behavior. Oil refining shutdown → Events that "have never happened" do not exist in data.

In domains rich with historical data — financial markets, consumer behavior, language patterns — AI is indeed powerful. But these are all domains where the future is an extension of the past.

When AI works:
Past patterns apply to the future → AI is effective
Unprecedented change occurs → AI is powerless
The problems we face now:
Fossil resource depletion → First time in human history
Large-scale ecosystem collapse → First time in human history
End of a civilization dependent on oil refining → First time in human history
→ The answers are not in past data

Masanobu Fukuoka saw the problem of chemical fertilizer dependency 50 years ago. Dr. Christine Jones discovered the principles of Light Farming. These insights came not from analyzing past data but from the ability to see structure.

AI excels at "rapidly repeating past patterns." What humans need is "perceiving patterns that have never existed before." These are fundamentally different capabilities, and AGI cannot substitute the latter.

Current AI Is Already Being Used

Before debating AGI, look at what current AI is already doing.

AI's track record: Military — The Ukrainian military has deployed AI for drone control, intelligence analysis, and target selection in live combat. Current AI is already transforming the battlefield — no need to wait for AGI. Software development — Claude itself is developed using Claude. AI writes, tests, and improves AI code. Developer productivity has already multiplied several times over. Structural analysis — The analysis on this site was written in collaboration with AI (Claude). Oil refining structures, chemical fertilizer dependencies, fusion reactor materials — humans pose questions, AI organizes information and makes structure visible.

All of this is done by current AI — not AGI. Current AI is powerful enough and practical enough. There is no need to wait for AGI.

Can AGI Break Even?

"Once AGI arrives, everything changes." But no one talks about AGI's economics.

AGI's economic structure:
Achieving AGI → Requires far larger data centers than current AI
→ Data center construction costs → Trillions of yen (tens of billions of dollars)
→ Operating costs (electricity, cooling, maintenance, chip replacement) → Hundreds of billions to trillions of yen annually
→ To recoup this investment, how much will companies pay?

The question is this: you trap geniuses inside a data center, feed them massive past data, and they produce answers. How much will companies pay for those answers?

Current Claude AGI (hypothetical)
--- --- ---
Development & operating cost Large but manageable Orders of magnitude larger
Capabilities Code generation, analysis, translation, document creation Same + "smarter"
What companies will pay Tens to hundreds of dollars/month per user ?
Additional problems solved — **Unclear what they are**

With current Claude, you can write code, perform analysis, translate, and create documents. If AGI becomes "smarter," how much more will companies pay? If current AI is sufficient for most tasks, AGI's additional value may not justify its enormous additional cost.

AGI's profitability problem: Development costs → Trillions of yen (look at Google's and Microsoft's annual AI investments) Operating costs → Hundreds of billions to trillions of yen annually (electricity, cooling, semiconductors) Additional value → Only the delta from current AI The more domains where "current AI is enough" expand, the harder it becomes to recoup AGI's additional investment

And all of this runs inside data centers built from petrochemical materials. Since fossil resources are finite, AGI's economic model is doubly unsustainable.

AGI may complete the world of information.
But the data center running AGI is itself built from oil.
Past data holds no answers for the future.
Current AI is already powerful enough.
And repairing the broken physical world can only be done by human hands.
